Sunny Hills Private Limited (“Sunny Hills”, “we”, “us”, or “our”) is committed to protecting the privacy and dignity of Sunny Hills Retirement Home (“Green Gardens”) residents, their families, our staff, and visitors to our website. This Privacy Policy explains what personal data we collect, why we collect it, how we use and protect it, and the rights you have over your data under the Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023.

1. Scope of This Policy

This Policy applies to:

  • Residents of Sunny Hills Retirement Home (“Green Gardens”) and their health, medical, and daily care data;
  • Family members and guardians who access our Family Portal or communicate with us;
  • Visitors to our website and users of our enquiry forms;
  • Staff, caregivers, and contractors, to the extent their data is processed in connection with resident care;
  • Anyone who contacts us via phone, WhatsApp, email, or social media.

2. Personal Data We Collect

2.1 Data you provide directly

Category Examples
Identity Data Full name, date of birth, gender, photograph, government ID (Aadhaar/Passport/PAN)
Contact Data Address, phone number, WhatsApp number, email address, country of residence
Health & Medical Data Medical history, current diagnoses, medication records, allergies, mobility status, vitals (heart rate, blood pressure, SpO2, sleep), care level requirements
Family & Emergency Contact Data Names, relationships, contact details, and consent records of family members or guardians
Financial Data Billing address, payment records (processed via secure payment gateway and we do not store card numbers)
Communication Data Messages sent via our enquiry form, WhatsApp, email, or social media

2.2 Data Collected Automatically or via Technology

Category Examples
CCTV Footage Video recordings from cameras placed in common areas, corridors, and entry points (never in private rooms or bathrooms)
Wearable Device Data Heart rate, sleep quality, activity levels, and fall-detection alerts from resident wearables
Website Usage Data IP address, browser type, pages visited, time spent, via cookies and analytics tools
Smart Access Data Entry/exit logs from biometric access control and visitor management systems

3. How We Use Your Data

We process personal data only for the following purposes, and only to the extent necessary:

  • To provide and coordinate resident care, including medical, nutritional, and daily living support;
  • To communicate with families through the Family Portal, WhatsApp updates, and AI-generated report cards;
  • To operate emergency response systems (panic buttons, fall detection, nurse alerts);
  • To process admissions, billing, and contractual obligations under the Resident Admission Agreement;
  • To comply with legal and regulatory obligations (health authorities, fire safety, labour law);
  • To respond to enquiries submitted via our website or social media;
  • To improve our services through aggregated, anonymised analytics;
  • To send updates about Sunny Hills (“Green Gardens”), where you have opted in to such communication.

4. Legal Basis for Processing

Under the DPDP Act, 2023, we process personal data on the following grounds:

  • Consent: For most data processing, including health data, family portal access, and marketing communications, we rely on your free, informed, and specific consent.
  • Legitimate Use: For limited purposes such as responding to your enquiry or fulfilling a contract you have requested, in accordance with Section 7 of the DPDP Act.
  • Legal Obligation: Where processing is required to comply with applicable law (e.g., health authority reporting, labour law compliance).

5. Consent for Residents Lacking Capacity

Where a resident is unable to provide informed consent due to a medical condition (such as advanced dementia), we obtain consent from their lawful guardian or the family member authorised under the Resident Admission Agreement, in accordance with Section 9 of the DPDP Act governing data principals who require assistance. Consent is documented in writing and may be withdrawn at any time by the authorised guardian.

6. CCTV and Surveillance

We maintain surveillance to ensure security and safety across our sanctuary:

  • Cameras are installed only in common areas, corridors, dining halls, and entry/exit points.
  • Cameras are never installed in private bedrooms, bathrooms, or changing areas.
  • Footage is used solely for resident safety, incident review, and anomaly detection.
  • Footage is retained for a maximum of 30 days unless required for an active investigation, after which it is automatically deleted.
  • Access to footage is restricted to authorised security and care management personnel.

7. Sharing of Personal Data

We do not sell personal data. We share personal data only in the following circumstances:

Recipient Purpose
Authorised Family Members Health updates, photos, and activity reports, as per consent given at admission
Technology Vendors CRM provider, cloud hosting, WhatsApp Business API, wearable platform and each bound by a Data Processing Agreement
Medical Professionals Doctors, hospitals, and specialists involved in a resident's care, on a need-to-know basis
Regulatory Authorities Where legally required (health department, police, court order)
Insurance Providers Where a resident or family has opted into insurance-linked services

8. Data Storage, Security, and Retention

  • All data is stored on secure, access-controlled cloud servers with encryption in transit and at rest.
  • Access to resident health data is restricted to authorised care staff on a need-to-know basis.
  • Resident data is retained for the duration of residency and for 8 years thereafter, in line with medical record-keeping norms, after which it is securely deleted or anonymised.
  • Website enquiry data is retained for 2 years from the last contact, unless an active relationship continues.
  • In the event of a data breach likely to cause harm, we will notify affected individuals and the Data Protection Board of India in accordance with the DPDP Act and other regulations if any applicable at the time.

9. Your Rights as a Data Principal

Under the DPDP Act, 2023, you (or your authorised guardian) have the right to:

  • Access a summary of the personal data we hold about you and how it is processed;
  • Correct, complete, or update inaccurate or outdated personal data;
  • Erase personal data that is no longer necessary for the purpose it was collected, subject to legal retention requirements;
  • Withdraw consent at any time, without affecting the lawfulness of processing before withdrawal;
  • Nominate another individual to exercise these rights on your behalf in the event of death or incapacity;
  • Lodge a complaint with the Data Protection Board of India if you believe your rights have been violated.

To exercise any of these rights, contact our Grievance Officer using the details in Section 11.

10. Cookies and Website Analytics

Our website uses cookies and similar technologies to improve functionality and understand visitor behaviour. You can control cookie preferences through your browser settings. We use Google Analytics 4 and similar tools in an anonymised, aggregated form wherever possible.
